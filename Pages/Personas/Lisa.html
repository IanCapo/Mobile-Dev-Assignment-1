Who is Lisa?

'I'd like to be able to wake up as late as possible without being late to work.'

Lisa from Birmingham, UK works as a marketing manager in central Birmingham. Her apartment is located just beyond the city limits and the commuting time varies between 30 and 60 minutes depending on the traffic.

Lisa is a night owl and stays up until late but she also loves to sleep.

How would she use the app?

Lisa opens the app, and finds herself on the main screen. It shows a toggle button for geolocation, a clock and a button with a plus at the bottom of the page.

On the main screen Lisa clicks on the '+'-button at the bottom of the page. The screen shows a form with various input fields.

On the current page Lisa finds options for starting point and destination. For the starting point she has the option to use geolocation or to type in the address. She decides to type both adresses. The input fields are smart and recognise what she types. She easily finds both her home address and her work address.

The next input is a digital clock, Lisa chooses 8:45am as her prefered arrival time.

Lisa needs 45 minutes to get ready for work. She scrolls through the digital clock and leaves it at 00:45.

The next option offers a repeated alarm. As Lisa always starts working at 9am she toggles it to yes. Checkboxes with the days of the week appear. She marks Monday to Friday.

Lisa has the possibility to name the alarm. She starts typing 'wo...', this input is smart too and offers the option 'work', she taps on it.

Now the only thing Lisa needs to do is confirm by tapping on the button at the bottom of the page that says 'Set alarm'. A modal appears, it says: "To turn off alarm shake you phone or swipe to the left."

Traffic is heavy today, the alarm rings at 7am. Lisa turns of the alarm by shaking her phone.
